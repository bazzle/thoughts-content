"Chancellor on brink of second bailout for banks" read the front page of The Times on January 3, 2009. That day, the psuedonymous Bitcoin founder Satoshi Nakamoto mined the first ever Bitcoin block, block zero, the Genesis block. And in an undeniably blatant slap-in-your-face notice of intent to go to war with a particular adversary, they embedded this headline into the block itself. That adversary is, of course the traditional financial system, which had in the preceding weeks so spectacularly imploded under the pressure of absurdly idiotic monetary policy decisions, made by banksters behind closed doors and the politicians and regulators who were turned blind eyes and willingly persued the path of maximum profit at any costs.

The years prior to the 2008 crash, and the ensuing years, where the same mistakes are being made as I write, albeit with a slightly different flavour — have taught us that humans are fundamentally incapable of making the decisions that are beneficial for the longer term and the greater good, and will always favour short termism – the quick buck.
